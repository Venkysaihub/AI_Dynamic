What Is Car Hire Insurance Coverage?

Car hire insurance coverage ensures that you are protected in case of damage to the rental vehicle or an accident during your hire period. It may include options like collision damage, theft protection, or third-party liability insurance.

1. Reviewing Your Rental Insurance Options

Rental insurance is a common benefit offered by car hire services, covering damage to the vehicle and other incidents like theft or third-party liability.

How to Check for Insurance Coverage:

  • Look for terms related to "Collision Damage Waiver (CDW)," "Theft Protection," or "Third-Party Liability."
  • Understand the coverage limits and excess amounts specified in your rental agreement.

How to Check for Repair Coverage:

3. Third-Party Liability Insurance

Third-party liability insurance covers you in the event of damage to another vehicle or property. This coverage is often included in rental agreements but should be verified.

How to Check for Third-Party Liability Coverage:

  • Ensure that third-party liability is included for protection in case of an accident with another vehicle or property.
  • Confirm if the coverage includes injury or damage to other people involved.

4. Exclusions and Limitations in Your Policy

Be aware of exclusions and limitations in your car hire policy, such as specific situations not covered or distance limitations for damages.

Common Exclusions:

  • Damage caused by negligence or driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs.
  • Exclusions for damage to the vehicle that occurs outside of normal driving conditions.
