Top Winter Car Recovery Tips

To help avoid winter breakdowns and ensure quick recovery if one occurs, follow these essential tips:

  • Check Your Battery: Cold temperatures can drain car batteries. Ensure your battery is in good condition, and replace it if it's old or showing signs of wear.
  • Inspect Your Tyres: Make sure your tyres have enough tread to handle slippery roads. Consider switching to winter tyres for better grip on icy and snowy surfaces.
  • Keep Your Fuel Tank Full: A full fuel tank prevents condensation, which can cause your fuel line to freeze. Aim to keep your tank at least half full during winter months.
  • Prepare for Emergencies: Pack an emergency kit with essentials such as a blanket, gloves, a torch, non-perishable food, and a phone charger in case you get stuck in severe weather.
  • Check Fluid Levels: Ensure your car has sufficient antifreeze and windshield washer fluid. These fluids are crucial in freezing conditions.
