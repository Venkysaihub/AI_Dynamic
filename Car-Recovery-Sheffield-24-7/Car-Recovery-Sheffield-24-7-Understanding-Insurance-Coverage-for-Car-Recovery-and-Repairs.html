What Is Car Recovery Insurance Coverage?

Car recovery coverage is an essential part of your car insurance policy that helps cover the cost of towing or transporting your car after a breakdown or accident. This can include roadside assistance, towing to a garage, and in some cases, repairs.

1. Check Your Insurance Policy for Roadside Assistance

Many car insurance policies offer roadside assistance, which can cover towing, flat tires, fuel shortages, or other breakdown-related issues. Make sure you know what is included in your policy before you need to call for help.

How to Check for Roadside Assistance:

  • Look for terms like “towing,” “roadside assistance,” or “emergency recovery” in your policy.
  • Understand towing limits and distances specified in your policy.

2. Understand Repair Coverage in Your Policy

Some insurance policies include repair coverage after accidents or breakdowns. Comprehensive insurance typically covers both recovery and repairs, while third-party insurance may only cover damages to other vehicles.

How to Check for Repair Coverage:

  • Comprehensive vs. Third-Party Coverage: Comprehensive covers both recovery and repairs, while third-party coverage might not include repairs or recovery.
  • Check the deductibles and limits on repair costs in your policy.
