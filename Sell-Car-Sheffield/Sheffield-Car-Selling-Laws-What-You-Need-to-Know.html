1. Vehicle Ownership and Proof of Ownership

Before selling your car, ensure that you have all the required documents to prove ownership. In Sheffield, the most important document is the Vehicle Registration Certificate (V5C), which shows that you're the registered keeper of the car.

Documents required for selling:
  • V5C registration certificate
  • Service history and MOT records (if available)
  • Proof of ID and address (for personal sales)

3. Consumer Protection and Legal Rights

In Sheffield, consumer protection laws apply if you're selling to a private buyer. Under the Sale of Goods Act 1979, the vehicle must match the description and be of satisfactory quality. If the car has serious issues after the sale, the buyer may be entitled to a refund or compensation.

Key legal points for private sellers:
  • The car must match its description
  • Be aware of the buyer's legal right to request repairs or compensation for defects

4. Transferring Ownership and the DVLA

Once the sale is complete, you must inform the DVLA of the ownership transfer. This can be done online or by post using the V5C registration certificate. Failure to notify the DVLA can result in penalties or continued liability for the vehicle.

Ownership transfer steps:
  • Complete the relevant section of the V5C and notify the DVLA
  • If selling to a dealer, the dealer will handle the transfer for you
