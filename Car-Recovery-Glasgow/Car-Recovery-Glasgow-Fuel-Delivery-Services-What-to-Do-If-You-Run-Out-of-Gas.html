1. Recognizing the Signs Before You Run Out of Fuel

Before you run out of gas, it's important to monitor your vehicle’s fuel levels. Ignoring the low fuel light or pushing the limits of your fuel gauge can result in an inconvenient situation.

  • Monitor Your Fuel Gauge: Check the gauge regularly, especially on longer trips.
  • Fuel Up Early: Refuel when your tank is about a quarter full.
  • Know Your Car’s Range: Each vehicle has a different fuel range on empty; don’t take risks.

Despite precautions, you may still run out of gas, so here’s what to do next.

2. What to Do If You Run Out of Gas

If you run out of fuel, remain calm and prioritize safety.

  • Pull Over Safely: Move your vehicle to the shoulder or a safe spot.
  • Turn on Hazard Lights: Make your car visible to other drivers, especially in low-visibility situations.
  • Assess Your Location: Try to estimate your distance to the nearest gas station or safe area.

3. When to Call for Fuel Delivery Services

  • Safety Concerns: Transporting fuel by foot can be dangerous.
