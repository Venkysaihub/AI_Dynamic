1. Plan Early and Set a Timeline

Start planning your office move well in advance. Define key dates, such as lease end and start dates at the new location, and create a timeline for each moving phase. Early planning helps avoid downtime and disruption to your business operations.

2. Conduct a Workplace Audit

Assess your current office layout, furniture, and equipment. Decide what needs to be moved, sold, donated, or disposed of. Streamlining your inventory can reduce moving costs and create a fresh start in your new workspace.

4. Communicate with Your Team

5. Prepare IT and Communication Systems

Coordinate with your IT department or provider to safely disconnect, move, and set up all computers, servers, and phone systems. Minimising downtime is critical for business continuity.
