Emergency Guide: What to Do When Your Phone Gets Water Damaged

Accidentally dropped your phone in water? Whether it fell in the sink, toilet, or a puddle, water damage can be a serious issue. Acting fast can increase your chances of saving your device. In this guide, we'll outline the immediate steps you should take and how Phone Shop Sheffield can help with expert water damage repairs.

Step 1: Turn Off Your Phone Immediately

Power down your phone as soon as possible to prevent short circuits. Do not press buttons or attempt to check if it’s still working—this can push water further into the internal components.

Step 2: Remove External Components

If possible, take out your SIM card, memory card, and any accessories. If your phone has a removable battery, remove it as well to reduce the risk of internal damage.

Step 3: Dry the Exterior

Use a soft, dry cloth or paper towel to gently blot away excess water. Do not shake the phone or use heat sources like hairdryers, as they can force moisture deeper into the device.

Step 4: Avoid Common Myths

Placing your phone in rice is a common myth, but it's not the most effective solution. Rice may absorb some moisture, but it doesn't remove all the trapped water, and rice particles can enter your phone, causing further problems. Instead, let the phone air-dry in a cool, dry place.

Step 5: Seek Professional Repair Services

Even if your phone appears to work after drying, internal components may still be corroding. The best way to ensure long-term functionality is to get it checked by professionals.
