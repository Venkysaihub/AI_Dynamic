What Are Electrical Safety Certificates?

Electrical safety certificates, such as the Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR), are official documents that confirm the safety and compliance of your electrical installations. These certificates are issued by qualified electricians after a thorough inspection and testing of your property’s electrical systems. The Risks of Not Having an Electrical Safety Certificate

  • Risk of Electrical Fires: Faulty wiring or outdated electrical systems can lead to fires, putting your property and safety at risk.
  • Personal Safety Concerns: Without proper certification, you may be unaware of electrical hazards that could cause shocks or other injuries.
  • Legal Consequences: Landlords and business owners are legally required to have valid electrical safety certificates. Non-compliance can result in fines or legal action.
  • Insurance Issues: Many insurance providers require valid electrical safety certificates to process claims. Without one, you may not be covered in case of an electrical incident.
