What Are Electrical Safety Certificates?

Electrical safety certificates, such as the Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR), are official documents that confirm the safety and compliance of your electrical installations. These certificates are issued by qualified electricians after a thorough inspection and testing of your property’s electrical systems. Why Are Electrical Safety Certificates Important?

  • Safety: They ensure your electrical systems are safe to use, reducing the risk of electrical fires, shocks, and other hazards.
  • Compliance: They help you meet legal requirements, especially for landlords and business owners.
  • Peace of Mind: Knowing your property is electrically safe provides confidence and security for you and your occupants.
  • Insurance: Many insurance providers require valid electrical safety certificates to process claims.

Types of Electrical Safety Certificates

  • Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR): This is the most common type of certificate, assessing the condition of your electrical installations and identifying any issues.
  • Portable Appliance Testing (PAT): This certificate ensures the safety of portable electrical appliances, such as kettles, computers, and extension leads.
  • Minor Electrical Installation Works Certificate: This is issued for small electrical works, such as adding a new socket or light fixture.
